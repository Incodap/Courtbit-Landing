Frequently Asked Questions

What is a padel round robin league?

A padel round robin (also known as "liga individual" or "mixto") is a league format where individual players sign up alone rather than as fixed pairs. Each match day, players are rotated into different partnerships, so everyone plays with and against everyone else over the course of the season. This format is great for meeting people and improving your game by adapting to different playing styles.

How does CourtBit generate round robin rotations?

CourtBit uses mathematical algorithms to create groups of 4 players with 3 rotations per group. In each rotation, players switch partners so that by the end of the group, everyone has played with and against everyone. The algorithm also respects player availability, so no one gets scheduled when they can't play.

Does CourtBit work for Americano or Mexicano format?

CourtBit is optimized for ongoing leagues with multiple match days over a season. For single-day Americano/Mexicano tournaments, there are other tools better suited for that format. However, many organizers use CourtBit for their season league and other tools for one-off events.
